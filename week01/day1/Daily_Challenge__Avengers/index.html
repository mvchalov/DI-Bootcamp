Created by the brilliant mind of Tim Burton, Wednesday takes viewers on a thrilling and darkly comedic journey through the life of Wednesday Addams, the iconic character from the Addams Family.

Starring Jenna Ortega as Wednesday, the series is a coming-of-age story that explores the challenges of growing up in a world that is both fascinating and terrifying. With a stellar cast that includes Christina Ricci, Catherine Zeta-Jones, Luis Guzmán, and Tim Burton himself, Wednesday has quickly become one of the most talked-about shows on television.

Wednesday Addams

Portrayed by Jenna Ortega, Wednesday is a teenage girl with a penchant for the macabre and the supernatural.

Sent to Nevermore Academy after causing trouble at several other schools, she finds herself investigating a series of murders that forces her to confront her own identity and her family's secrets. Wednesday is a resourceful and fiercely independent character, embodying the spirit of rebellion and individualism.

Enid Sinclair

Enid is an energetic, excitable and optimistic teenager. She is very caring towards her friends and is one of the few who actually bond with Wednesday.

However, she hides an insecure side about her total transformation into a werewolf and is pressured by her mother. She is also very loyal to Wednesday whom she considers a true friend, which is shown when she is the first to want to reconcile with her after arguments over their differences and the events on the show. She is portrayed by Emma Myers.

Tyler Galpin

Tyler Galpin is the son of Sheriff Donovan Galpin and his late wife Francoise and an antagonist of the first season of Wednesday.

After his birth, his mother, Francoise, went through postpartum depression, which then led to her Hyde being unlocked. His mother died at some point afterwards. His mother was a Hyde, like Tyler, and his father was worried it had been passed on, so he doesn't like to talk about her.

Xavier Thorpe

Xavier Thorpe is a psychic student at Nevermore Academy and a friend of Wednesday Addams. Xavier is a psychic artist and possesses an innate talent for painting, which he can animate through his telekinesis.

He's one of the more notable students at Nevermore; he appears in every episode and was one of the suspects Wednesday had when she was investigating the murders that happened to Jericho.

Bianca Barclay

Bianca Barclay (born Brandy Jane) is a siren student at Nevermore Academy, the daughter of Gabrielle, Xavier Thorpe's ex-girlfriend.

She is initially Wednesday's rival during her tenure at Nevermore; however, they eventually manage to leave their rivalry behind and work together to resolve the crimes in their school. Bianca plays a significant role in the final confrontation of the season.

Marilyn Thornhill

Marilyn Thornhill (born Laurel Gates) is a normie and also the main antagonist of the Netflix series Wednesday. She was formerly Wednesday Addams' and Enid Sinclair's dorm mom and botanical sciences teacher at Nevermore Academy.

Descendant of Joseph Crackstone, Marilyn faked her death in order to change her identity and accomplish Crackstone's task: eradicating the outcasts.

Larissa Weems

Principal Larissa Weems is a shapeshifter and a former roommate of Morticia Addams when they both were at school

She was highly passionate about Nevermore Academy and devoted all of her time to the faculty and students. She put all of her effort into keeping the school a sanctuary for outcasts and making sure that the reputation of the school wasn't tainted in any way.

Episodes

Fans of the series can't get enough of Wednesday's wit, charm, and unapologetic attitude. Whether she's navigating the social hierarchy of Nevermore Academy, uncovering dark secrets about her family's past, or battling supernatural forces, Wednesday always manages to come out on top.

Wednesday's Child Is Full of WoeEpisode 1

When a deliciously wicked prank gets Wednesday expelled, her parents ship her off to Nevermore Academy, the boarding school where they fell in love.

Woe Is the Loneliest NumberEpisode 2

The sheriff questions Wednesday about the night's strange happenings. Later, Wednesday faces off against a fierce rival in the cutthroat Poe Cup race.

Friend or WoeEpisode 3

Wednesday stumbles on a secret society. During Outreach Day, Nevermore's outcasts mingle with Jericho's normies in Pilgrim World. Fudge, anyone?

Woe What a NightEpisode 4

Wednesday asks Xavier to the Rave'N dance, sparking Tyler's jealousy - but Thing's got something up his sleeve. Meanwhile, Eugene stakes out the cave.

You Reap What You WoeEpisode 5

During Parents Weekend, Wednesday digs into her family's past - and accidentally gets her dad arrested. Enid feels the pressure to "wolf out."

Quid Pro WoeEpisode 6

Wednesday's friends throw her a surprise birthday party. They mean well - but she'd much rather mark the miserable occasion by solving the murders.

If You Don't Woe Me by NowEpisode 7

Kooky Uncle Fester pays a visit and shares his theory about the monster. Wednesday begrudgingly agrees to a date with Tyler at Crackstone's crypt.

A Murder of WoesEpisode 8

Wednesday lands in trouble with Principal Weems, but that's just the start of her problems. To fight an ancient evil, she'll need all her friends' help.
